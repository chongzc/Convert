4.                   Shareholders’ Obligations and Shareholders’ Advances

4.1               Shareholders’ Obligations

4.1.2         the Company will comply with the provisions of this Agreement and the Constitution and will act in such manner and achieve the full intent and purpose of this Agreement;

4.1.3         the business of the Company shall be carried on pursuant to the policies set out herein or laid down from time to time by the Board;

4.1.4         the Company shall keep full and proper accounting records in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles relating to its business, undertakings and affairs, which records shall be made available at all reasonable times for inspection by the Directors and/or the Shareholders by prior appointment (at least five (5) Business Days’ notice) during office hours;

4.1.5         the Company shall prepare annual financial statements, in each case in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles and in compliance with all applicable legislation in respect of each accounting reference period, and shall procure that such financial statements are audited as soon as practicable and shall supply copies of the same, both in draft and final form, to each of the Shareholders as soon as practicable after their issue;

5.                   Board of Directors

 

5.1               Directors: The Board shall initially comprise of the persons whose names are set out in Item 3(a) of the Schedule 3. For the avoidance of doubt, any further appointments and/or removal of such Directors shall be at the discretion of the nominating Shareholder.

5.2               Right of Appointment and Removal: The right of appointment conferred on a Shareholder under Clause 5.1 shall include the right of the Shareholder at any time and from time to time:

5.2.1         to remove such person appointed by that Shareholder as a Director; and

5.2.2         to determine the period during which such person shall hold the office of Director.

5.3               Chairman: The person whose name is set out in Item 3(b) of the Schedule 3 shall be the chairman of the Board of Directors. The chairman’s entitlement to a second casting vote shall subject to Item 3(c) of the Schedule 3.

5.4               Notice in Writing: Each appointment or removal of a Director pursuant to Clause 5.2 shall be in writing and signed by or on behalf of the Shareholder concerned and shall be delivered to the registered office for the time being of the Company.

5.5               Further Director: Whenever for any reason a person appointed by a Shareholder ceases to be a Director, that Shareholder shall be entitled to appoint forthwith another Director.

5.6               Alternate Director: A Director shall be entitled at any time and from time to time to appoint any person to act as its alternate and to terminate the appointment of such person and in that connection, the provisions of the Constitution shall be complied with. Such alternate director shall be entitled while holding office as such to receive notices of Board Meetings and to attend and vote as a Director at any such meetings at which the Director appointing it is not present and generally to exercise all the powers, rights, duties and authorities and to perform all functions of its appointer as the Director appointing it. Further, such alternate director shall be entitled to exercise the vote of the Director appointing it at any Board Meetings and if such alternate director represents more than one (1) Director, such alternate director shall be entitled to one (1) vote for every Director it represents. Each Director may also attend any Board Meetings with its alternate director but only the Director itself is entitled to vote at such meetings.

 

5.7               Committees: Any of the Directors may delegate any of its powers, including the day-to-day running of the business to a committee or committees consisting of such persons as it deems fit.

5.8               Board Meetings

5.8.1         The Directors shall hold Board Meetings at such time, place and frequency as the Board may decide from time to time. Any Director may call for a Board Meeting.

5.8.2         Each of the Directors shall be entitled to receive not less than five (5) Business Days’ written notice of all Board Meetings (or such shorter period of notice in respect of any particular Board Meeting as may be agreed jointly by all the Directors) specifying the date, time and place of the Board Meeting and the business to be transacted thereat.

5.8.3         The quorum at a Board Meeting or adjourned Board Meeting necessary for the transaction of any business of the Company shall be such number(s) of Directors as more particularly set out in Item 2(a) of the Schedule 3. In the event that a Board Meeting duly convened cannot be held for lack of quorum within thirty (30) minutes of the time appointed for the Board Meeting, the Board Meeting shall be adjourned to the same time and day of the following week and at the same place and at least five (5) Business Days’ notice shall be given to the Directors in relation to such adjourned Board Meeting.

5.8.4         If the quorum for a Board Meeting is not present within thirty (30) minutes of the time appointed for the adjourned Board Meeting, the adjourned Board Meeting shall be dissolved.

5.8.5         All resolutions of the Directors at a Board Meeting or adjourned Board Meeting shall be adopted by the voting method as more particularly described in Item 2(b) of the Schedule 3.

5.8.6         A resolution in writing signed by such number of Director(s) as more particularly set out in Item 2(a) of the Schedule 3 for the time being or their alternates shall be as valid and effectual as if it had been passed at a Board Meeting duly called and constituted. Any such resolution may consist of several documents in like form, each signed such number(s) of Directors as more particularly set out in Item 2(a) of the Schedule 3. The expressions “in writing” and “signed” include approval by wireless or facsimile transmission.

5.8.7         The Directors may participate in a Board Meeting by means of a conference telephone or a video conference telephone or similar communications equipment by which all persons participating in the Board Meeting are able to hear and be heard by all other participants without the need for a Director to be in the physical presence of another Director(s) and participation in the Board Meeting in this manner shall be deemed to constitute presence in person at such Board Meeting. The Directors participating in any such Board Meeting shall be counted in the quorum for such Board Meeting and subject to there being a requisite quorum under Clause 5.8.3 at all times during such Board Meeting, all resolutions agreed by the Directors in such Board Meeting shall be deemed to be as effective as a resolution passed at a Board Meeting attended in person by the Directors and duly convened and held.

5.8.8         A Board Meeting conducted by means of a conference telephone or a video conference telephone or similar communications equipment as aforesaid is deemed to be held at the place agreed upon by the Directors attending the Board Meeting, provided that at least one (1) of the Directors present at the Board Meeting was at that place for the duration of the Board Meeting.

6.                   General Meetings of Shareholders

6.1               Quorum and Voting

6.1.1         Unless longer notice is required by the Act, at least fourteen (14) days’ notice (in the case where only ordinary resolutions are to be passed) or at least twenty-one (21) days’ notice (in the case where special resolutions are to be passed) must be given before a General Meeting is convened (or such shorter period of notice in respect of any General Meeting as may be agreed by all the Shareholders) specifying the date, time and place of the General Meeting and the business to be transacted thereat.

6.1.2         The quorum at a General Meeting, or adjourned General Meeting necessary for the transaction of any business of the Company shall be a minimum of such number of Shareholder(s) as more particularly described in Item 4(a) of the Schedule 3, who shall be personally present or by their representatives. In the event that a General Meeting duly convened cannot be held for lack of a quorum within thirty (30) minutes of the time appointed for the General Meeting, the General Meeting shall be adjourned to the same time and day of the following week and at the same place and at least seven (7) days’ notice shall be given to the Shareholders in relation to such adjourned General Meeting.

6.1.3         If the quorum for a General Meeting is not present within thirty (30) minutes of the time appointed for the adjourned General Meeting, the adjourned General Meeting shall be dissolved.

6.1.4         Subject to any additional requirements specified by the Act and, all resolutions of the Shareholders in a General Meeting shall be adopted by such voting method as more particularly described in Item 4(b) of the Schedule 3 and on the basis that each Share will carry one (1) vote.

6.1.5         Subject to any additional requirements specified by the Act, and subject to Clause 6.1.4, an ordinary resolution in writing signed by at least one (1) of the Shareholder holding more than fifty per cent (50%) of the total Shares shall be as valid and effectual as if it had been passed as ordinary resolution at a General Meeting duly called and constituted. Any such ordinary resolution may consist of several documents in like form, each signed by one (1) or more of the Shareholders. The expressions “in writing” and “signed” include approval by wireless or facsimile transmission.

6.1.6         The Shareholders may participate in a General Meeting by means of a conference telephone or a video conference telephone or similar communications equipment by which all persons participating in the General Meeting are able to hear and be heard by all other participants without the need for a Shareholder to be in the physical presence of another Shareholder(s) and participation in the General Meeting in this manner shall be deemed to constitute presence in person at such General Meeting. 9.                   Transfer of Shares

9.1               Moratorium on Transfer: None of the Shareholders shall during the term of this Agreement sell, transfer, assign, charge, mortgage, grant options over or otherwise dispose of or encumber any of its Shares or any interest therein unless with the prior written approval of the other Shareholders and subject to compliance with the provisions of this Agreement.

9.2               Lock-in Period

9.2.1         There shall be a lock-in period of such number of months or years (as the case may be) as more particularly set out in Item 5 of the Schedule 3 (“Lock-in Period”).

9.3               Restriction on Transfer

9.3.1         Subject to Clause 9.6, none of the Shareholders shall transfer all or any part of the Shares held by it or otherwise sell, dispose of or deal with all or any part of its interest in such Shares unless and until the rights of first refusal conferred by this Clause 9.3 have been exhausted.

9.3.2         None of the Shareholders shall, without the prior written consent of the other Shareholders, create or have outstanding any Encumbrance or security interest on or over any Shares or any part of its interest in such Shares (otherwise than by a transfer of such Shares in accordance with this Agreement).

9.3.3         Any Shareholder who desires to transfer any Share or Shares (“Transferor”) shall give a notice to the Company and other Shareholders in proportion to their respective Shareholding Percentages of such desire to transfer the Sale Shares (“Transfer Notice”), which notice shall specify:

(i)             the number of Shares proposed to be sold and transferred (“Sale Shares”);

(ii)           such price as more particularly set out in Item 6(a) of the Schedule 3 for any sale during the Lock-in Period; OR such price as more particularly set out in Item 6(b) of the Schedule 3 for any sale after the Lock-in Period; OR if there is no Lock-in Period in this Agreement, such price as more particularly set out in Item 6(b) of the Schedule 3 for any sale; provided that if the Fair Value of such Shares is zero or a negative amount, the purchase price for all the Shares shall be Ringgit Malaysia One (RM1.00) only (“Transferor’s Price”);

(iii)          the other terms and conditions of such sale (if any) (“Prescribed Terms”); and

(iv)         the identity of the person to whom the Transferor proposes to transfer such Shares (“Buyer”).

9.3.4         Subject as hereinafter mentioned, a Transfer Notice shall constitute an offer by the Transferor for the sale of the Sale Shares to Shareholders and on the Prescribed Terms (if any). A Transfer Notice shall not be revocable except with the sanction of the Board.

9.3.5         The offer for sale contained in the Transfer Notice must be accepted within fifteen (15) Business Days of the receipt of the Transfer Notice (“Acceptance Period”).

9.3.6         If the offer for sale contained in the Transfer Notice is not accepted within the Acceptance Period or is rejected during such Period, then the Transferor may offer to sell the Sale Shares to the Buyer within forty-five (45) Business Days following the expiry of the Acceptance Period, or following the day on which the Transferor is served upon all the rejection notices in writing from each of the Shareholders (whichever is earlier).

9.3.7         The sale of any Shares specified in the transfer notice to the buyer must be at a price not less than the Transferor’s Price and on terms not more favourable than the Prescribed Terms contained in the Transfer Notice.

9.3.8         On or before the Transferor’s transfer of all its Shares in the Company to the Buyer:

14.               Deadlock

14.1           Deadlock

 

14.1.1     Subject always to the provisions of Clause 14.3 below, a deadlock is deemed to have occurred if no resolution is reached on any matter at two (2) consecutive General Meetings and such non-approval of the matter has a Material Adverse Effect on the Company (“Deadlock”).

 

For the purpose of this Clause 14.1.1, “Material Adverse Effect” means any event, condition or change which materially and adversely affects or could reasonably be expected to materially and adversely affect the business, assets, properties, liabilities, financial results of operations, conditions (financial or otherwise) or prospects of the Company.

 

14.2           Mechanism for Resolving Deadlock

 

14.2.1     To resolve a Deadlock, any Shareholder may, by a seven (7) Business Days’ written notice to the other Shareholders, request a meeting on any Business Day not being more than fourteen (14) days after the date the Deadlock occurred. All the Shareholders shall attend such meeting.

14.2.2     The Shareholders undertake that they shall each use all endeavours in good faith to resolve the Deadlock amicably.

14.2.3     If the relevant Deadlock is not resolved within thirty (30) days from the date the meeting is convened pursuant to such notice, then the Shareholders may serve a Deadlock Notice on the Shareholder(s) who disagree with them, or who do not approve the resolution(s) proposed by them (“Disapproving Shareholder(s)”) in respect of the matter within a further thirty (30) days and the ensuing provisions of this Clause 14.2 will apply.

14.3           Deadlock Notice

 

14.3.1     The Shareholders who are entitled to serve a notice under Clause 14.2.3 may serve upon the Disapproving Shareholder(s) and the Company a notice in writing (“Deadlock Notice”) containing:

(i)                 a representation that the Shareholders (“Offeror Shareholders”) are, pursuant to the terms of Clause 14.2, entitled to serve such a notice;

(ii)                an offer by the Offeror Shareholders to purchase all the Shares held by the Disapproving Shareholder(s), in proportion to their respective Shareholding Percentages; and

(iii)              at a purchase price as more particularly set out in Item 7(a) of the Schedule 3 for any default occurs during the Lock-in Period; OR such price as more particularly set out in Item 7(b) of the Schedule 3 for any default occurs after the Lock-in Period; OR if there is no Lock-in Period in this Agreement, such price as more particularly set out in Item 7(b) of the Schedule 3 for any default occurs; provided that if the Fair Value of such Shares is zero or a negative amount, the purchase price for all the Shares shall be Ringgit Malaysia One (RM1.00) only (“Deadlock Price”).

14.4           Effect of Deadlock Notice

14.4.1     A Deadlock Notice shall constitute:

(i)                 an irrevocable offer by the notifying Offeror Shareholders, open for acceptance by the Disapproving Shareholder(s) for thirty (30) days from the date of service of the Deadlock Notice (“Deadlock Acceptance Period”) to purchase all (but not some only) of the Shares held by the Disapproving Shareholder(s) at the Deadlock Price (“Purchase Offer”);

(ii)                a representation that the Shares held by the Disapproving Shareholder(s) shall be bought, free from all Encumbrances and claims and together with all rights attaching thereto at the date of giving of the Deadlock Notice (“Deadlock Terms”); and

(iii)              a stipulation that any purchase of Shares from the Disapproving Shareholder(s) shall be conditional upon such Shares being sold by the Disapproving Shareholder(s) on the Deadlock Terms.

14.5           Purchase Offer / Buy-Sell Mechanism

14.5.1      At any time during the Deadlock Acceptance Period, the Disapproving Shareholder(s) may, by notice in writing (“Acceptance Notice”) to the Offeror Shareholders, accept the Purchase Offer. An Acceptance Notice shall constitute a binding obligation to purchase, at the Deadlock Price, all the Shares held by the Disapproving Shareholder(s) comprised in the Purchase Offer, which shall be transferred on the Deadlock Terms.

14.5.2      If no Acceptance Notice is served, the Disapproving Shareholder(s) shall be deemed to have given an irrevocable notice to the Offeror Shareholders offering to sell all the Shares held by the Disapproving Shareholder(s), and the Offeror Shareholders shall be bound to purchase all the Shares held by the Disapproving Shareholder(s) at the sum equivalent to Deadlock Price. (“Sale Offer”).

14.6           Completion

14.6.1      Completion of the sale and purchase of Shares pursuant to the Sale Offer or the Purchase Offer (as the case may be) shall take place on a day within sixty (60) days after:

(i)                 the date of receipt of the Acceptance Notice (in the case where the Disapproving Shareholder(s) accepts the Purchase Offer in accordance with the provisions of Clause 14.5.1); or

(ii)                the expiry of the Deadlock Acceptance Period (in the case where no Acceptance Notice is delivered by the Disapproving Shareholder(s), and the Disapproving Shareholder(s) shall be deemed to have given a Sale Offer in accordance with the provisions of Clause 14.5.2),

at the registered office of the Company, or such other date and place as the Shareholders may agree unanimously.

14.6.2     At completion:

    1.   – Fair Value

    In this Agreement where the Fair Value of any Shares (“Valuation Shares”) is required to be determined:

    (1)                The Shareholders will endeavour to agree the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares within seven (7) Business Days of the day on which pursuant to the provisions of this Agreement the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares was required to be determined.

    (2)                In default of the Shareholders agreeing to the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares in accordance with paragraph 1 of this Schedule 5, the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares (as on the day on which pursuant to the provisions of this Agreement the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares was required to be determined) as between a willing seller and a willing buyer contracting on arm’s length terms will be determined by an Auditor of the Company.

    (3)                In default of the Shareholders agreeing to the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares in accordance with paragraph 2 of this Schedule 5, the Company will appoint as valuer any firm of accountants or valuers who are not also Auditors of the Company, and such appointment shall be mutually agreed by the selling and purchasing shareholders.

    (4)                In determining and certifying the Fair Value of any Shares, a valuer, appointed by the Company pursuant to the provisions of paragraph 3, will take into account:

    (i)             the assets, including without limitation the goodwill of the Company; and

    (ii)            all such other circumstances as the valuer shall reasonably deem to be relevant and adequate.

    (5)                In determining and certifying the Fair Value of the relevant Shares, a valuer appointed by the Company pursuant to the provisions of paragraph 3, will value the Valuation Shares as a rateable proportion of all the issued Shares without discounting or enhancing the value by reference to the number of the Valuation Shares, i.e.:

    P   =   F.V.   x   N

    Where:

    P = the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares.

    F.V. = the Fair Value of one Valuation Share.

    N = the number of the Valuation Shares.

    (6)                A valuer, appointed by the Company pursuant to the provisions of paragraph 3, will in determining the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares afford the Shareholders the opportunity to make representations to them.

    (7)                A valuer, appointed by the Company pursuant to the provisions of paragraph 3, will in determining and certifying the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares act as experts and not as arbitrators and their decision will (save in respect of manifest error) be final and binding on the Shareholders, subject only to the right of the holder of the Valuation Shares to issue a notice under the provisions of paragraph 8 of this Schedule 5.

    (8)                If the holder of the Valuation Shares is dissatisfied with determination of the Fair Value by the first valuer appointed by the Company pursuant to the provisions of paragraph 3 of this Schedule 5, it may within 14 days of the date on which it receives notice of the first valuation notify the Company of its dissatisfaction and that they wish a second valuation of the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares to be conducted.

    (9)                If the Company receives a notice under and in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 8 of this Schedule 5, the Company will appoint a second valuer in accordance with the provisions of paragraph 3 of this Schedule 5.

    (10)            In the event two valuations are made in accordance with the provisions of this Schedule 5, the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares will be equal to the arithmetic mean of the two valuations.

    (11)            The fees and expenses of the first valuers in determining and certifying the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares will be borne by the Company while the fees and expenses of any second valuer in determining and certifying the Fair Value of the Valuation Shares will be borne by the Shareholder who requested the second valuation.
