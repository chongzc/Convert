11.   

Reserved Matters

all the shareholders shall procure that none of the following matters (“Reserved Matters”) shall be undertaken by the Company unless with the prior written approval of all the shareholders:

 

·              Any change in the nature and/or scope of the business of the Company.

·              The dissolution, liquidation, or winding-up of the Company.

·              Any amendment to the Company’s constitution.

·              Any increase in the share capital of the Company or the issue or grant of any option over the unissued share capital of the Company or the issue of any new class of shares in the capital of the Company or the issuing of any convertible securities by the Company.

·              Any repurchase, cancellation or redemption of the Company’s share capital or any reduction, consolidation, subdivision or reclassification or other alteration of its capital structure.

·              The exercise of the borrowing powers of the Company, other than borrowings approved in the Company’s annual budget.

·              The creation of any mortgage, charge or other encumbrance over the Company’s property.

·              The Company entering into, or varying, or waiving any breach of, or discharge of any liability under, or terminating, any contract or arrangement (whether legally binding or not) with any of its related corporations.

·              The declaration or payment of any dividends or other distribution of profits of the Company (whether in cash or in specie).

·              Any amalgamation or reconstruction of the Company, or any merger of the Company with any corporation, firm or other body.

·              Any disposal or the acquisition of, or investment in, any undertaking, assets (including, without limitation, copyright, trademarks, service marks, patents or other intellectual property rights and any interest in any land or real property) or shares or other equity interests by the Company.

·              The commencement, defence or settlement by the Company of any litigation, arbitration or administrative proceedings other than as plaintiff in the collection of debts arising in the ordinary course of business.

·              The appointment or removal of, or change in, the Company’s auditors.

·              The grant by the Company of any power of attorney.

·              The establishment of any committee of the Board, and delegation of any powers of the Board to any such committee.

·              Any public offering or listing or quotation of the shares or other equity of the Company on any stock exchange.

·              The provision of any credit, or the making of any loan (including any loans to the shareholders) or advance to, or for, any person, company or body, other than by way of deposit of moneys with a bank or other financial institution.

·              The exercise of the Company’s powers to provide guarantees or indemnities.

·              The approval of the annual budget (including any amendments, modifications, addendum or additions thereto) of the Company.

·              The approval of the audited financial statements (including any amendments, modifications, addendum or additions thereto) of the Company.

·              The incurring by the Company of any capital expenditure (including the acquisition of any undertaking or asset whether under lease or hire purchase or otherwise) other than capital expenditure approved in the annual budget.

·              The appointment of or any subsequent change in the key or senior personnel of the Company.

·              The appointment or removal of, or change in, the directors of the Company.

·              The approval of the remuneration (including salary, allowances and benefits) of the directors and/or key or senior personnel of the Company.

·              The establishment of any branch or representative office of the Company, or the entry by the Company into any partnership or joint venture or co-operation agreement with any other party.

·              The adoption of, or any significant change in, the accounting policies of the Company, other than as required by law or accounting policies generally accepted in Malaysia from time to time.

19.   

Deadlock

 

A deadlock is deemed to have occurred if no resolution is reached on any Reserved Matter at two (2) consecutive General Meetings and such non-approval of the Reserved Matter has a Material Adverse Effect on the Company

 

(“Material Adverse Effect” means any event, condition or change which materially and adversely affects or could reasonably be expected to materially and adversely affect the business, assets, properties, liabilities, financial results of operations, conditions (financial or otherwise) or prospects of the Company)

 

If the deadlock is not resolved within 30 days, any shareholders may issue a notice to purchase all the others shares/ sell its shares to other shareholders. If no notice is served, the company will be liquidated and dissolved.
